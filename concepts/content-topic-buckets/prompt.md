# Content Topic Buckets — BIZ-09

Use these four buckets to generate a balanced, repeatable content mix. Every content skill (`content-writing`, `content-social`, `biz-content`, `biz-youtube`) should rotate across them so the audience gets depth, credibility, and proof without repetition.

> **Rule of thumb:** If you can replace your name/company with anyone else's and the piece still works, it's too generic. Each bucket should be specific enough that only you could have created it.

---

## The four buckets

| Bucket | What it is | Best formats | Examples |
|--------|------------|--------------|----------|
| **Architecture lesson** | A design decision, tradeoff, mistake, or pattern — with the specifics. | Long-form post, carousel, YouTube tutorial, blog article | LinkedIn: "I chose PostgreSQL over MongoDB for a financial platform. Here's why — and what I almost got wrong." / YouTube: "Why I chose Postgres over Mongo for a finance platform" |
| **AI in practice** | Real experience with AI/ML — not hype, not theory. | Case study, behind-the-scenes, short demo | LinkedIn: "I built an AI Persona Clone with RAG + voice. Here's what actually worked and what didn't." / YouTube: "I built a voice clone with RAG. Here's what actually worked" |
| **Experience-based insight** | Judgment that only comes from years of doing this. | Thought leadership post, hot take, thread | LinkedIn: "I've seen 4 technology hype cycles. Here's what survives and what doesn't." / YouTube: "4 hype cycles later: what tech actually survives" |
| **Project behind-the-scenes** | Specific, credible, ships — show the work with numbers. | Build log, architecture tour, case study | LinkedIn: "I built a financial tax tracking and reporting platform solo in 7 months. Here's the architecture." / YouTube: "Solo-built a tax platform in 7 months: architecture tour" |

---

## How to use

1. **Pick one bucket per piece.** Don't combine two buckets in one post unless the format explicitly calls for it (e.g., a long-form case study can include an architecture lesson).
2. **Rotate buckets.** Never post the same bucket three times in a row. A healthy weekly mix for LinkedIn might be: Architecture lesson → Experience-based insight → Project behind-the-scenes.
3. **Match bucket to format.** Architecture lessons often become carousels or YouTube tutorials. Experience insights become short posts or tweets. Project BTS becomes long-form posts or videos.
4. **Derive the hook from the bucket.** The hook should make the bucket obvious in the first line.

---

## Platform-specific notes

- **LinkedIn / text posts:** Keep under 300 words. Lead with the single insight; use the body to prove it with one concrete detail.
- **LinkedIn carousel:** Frame the bucket as a numbered framework or lesson. Architecture lessons and experience insights work best.
- **YouTube long-form:** 8–15 minutes. Architecture lessons and project BTS are natural fits; AI-in-practice works when it includes demos.
- **YouTube Shorts:** 30–60 seconds. One bucket = one Short. Use experience insights and AI-in-practice for high-retention hooks.
- **Blog / newsletter:** Any bucket can expand to 1,000+ words. Add subheadings that map back to the bucket.
