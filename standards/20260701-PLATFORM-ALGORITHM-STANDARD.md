# PLATFORM-ALGORITHM-STANDARD

Binding content distribution rules per platform. All content skills (`content-writing`, `content-social`) must follow these. Violating platform-specific rules reduces organic reach — sometimes to zero.

## Cross-platform universal rules

1. Platform-native content ONLY — never cross-post identical content across platforms
2. Early engagement (first 60 min) is the strongest reach signal on every platform
3. Engagement quality (saves, shares, comments, DMs) > volume (passive likes, impressions)
4. Reply to comments in real time — it doubles as an algorithmic signal
5. Video/visual content consistently outperforms text-only
6. Adopt new platform features early — bonus reach from the algorithm
7. Consistency > frequency — regular cadence builds algorithmic trust
8. No engagement bait on any platform (any form of "like/RT/agree if...")

## LinkedIn — BUSINESS CRITICAL

Rule: **External links (GitHub, websites, articles) are DEMOTED in the post body.** The algorithm shows link-containing posts to far fewer people. Best practice:

1. Post body: no external links
2. First comment immediately after posting: the links
3. Format: "Links to [resource description]: [URLs]"
4. Operator must be reminded of this rule on every LinkedIn delivery

Additional rules:
- Carousels (document posts) = 3-5x higher reach than text-only
- Personal profile > company page for organic reach
- 3-5 hashtags, selective and relevant
- Dwell time tracked — substantive content rewarded
- Best time: Tue-Thu 8-10 AM or 12-1 PM local audience time
- Anti-patterns: "Agree?" posts (now penalized), "copy-paste this", same hashtags on every post

### LinkedIn creator challenges (#CreateImpact and similar)

Official LinkedIn consistency programs reward **expertise sharing**, not volume spam.

1. **Cadence:** 2–4 posts/week beats daily low-quality posts
2. **Format mix:** ≥1 carousel/week; text for stories; video optional
3. **Challenge hashtags:** Use program hashtag (e.g. `#CreateImpact`) on **one post per week**, not every post
4. **Feedback loop:** Log format + hook + impressions in `@biz-content status` after each post; adjust from data
5. **ICP alignment:** Run BIZ-08 LinkedIn ICP before starting; save to `.work.biz/strategy/linkedin-icp.md`
6. **Ops skill:** `@biz-content challenge` for 30-day plan; `@content-social write linkedin carousel` for highest-reach drafts

## Reddit

Rule: Each subreddit has its own culture and rules. Research before posting.

- 10:1 value-to-self-promo ratio is the safe zone
- Link-dropping without community engagement = deleted and banned
- Title quality is critical — it's the only thing 90% of viewers see
- Text posts often outperform link posts in discussion subreddits
- AMA-style and educational content valued
- Anti-patterns: marketing-speak, asking for upvotes, posting same link to multiple subs

## X (Twitter)

Rule: External links reduce reach but are not banned. Use sparingly.

- First tweet must hook in under 280 characters
- Threads (5-8 tweets) for depth; standalone for hot takes
- Semantic AI maps content to topic vectors — natural language wins
- Profile Reputation Score matters — engage with others regularly
- Best time: Tue-Fri 9 AM-1 PM local audience time
- Anti-patterns: engagement bait, massive hashtag blocks, cross-posted Instagram captions

## Instagram

Rule: **No clickable links in captions.** Only one link in bio. Use Stories link sticker or "link in bio" CTA.

- DM shares are the premier engagement signal — more valuable than likes
- Reels = highest reach (short-form video is primary discovery engine)
- Originality engine down-ranks reposted/watermarked/TikTok-recycled content
- Separate algorithms for Feed, Stories, Explore, Reels, Search
- 3-5 hashtags — avoid oversaturated tags
- Carousel posts > single images
- Stories prime your audience for feed posts
- Anti-patterns: recycled TikTok watermarks, 30-hashtag shotgun, static-image-only strategy

## Facebook

Rule: **External links are penalized.** Prefer native content. Links go in first comment.

- Groups have much higher organic reach than Pages
- Personal profiles > business pages for organic reach
- Native video (upload directly) gets highest reach — don't link to YouTube
- "Meaningful interactions" between people prioritized
- Best time: Wed-Thu 10 AM-3 PM local audience time
- Anti-patterns: link-dropping, engagement bait, over-posting
