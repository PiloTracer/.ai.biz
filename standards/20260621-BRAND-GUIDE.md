# Brand Guide

Binding rules for brand presence across all channels. All `biz-brand`, `biz-content`, `content-social`, `content-writing`, and `biz-youtube` output must comply.

## Core brand rule: one unified offer

1. The business must have **one primary offer** expressed in a single sentence.
2. That offer must appear:
   - In the LinkedIn headline (first 80 characters)
   - Above the fold on the website homepage
   - In the YouTube channel "About" section (first 140 characters)
   - In every piece of content at least implicitly
3. Never lead with multiple offers, a list of services, or generic titles ("Consultant | Advisor | Speaker").

## 5-second test

Any visitor to any profile or page must be able to answer within 5 seconds:

- What do you do?
- For whom?
- Why should they care?

If the answer is unclear, the asset fails. Fix the headline, banner, or hero before publishing content.

## LinkedIn

- Headline format: `[Result] for [Target buyer]` or `[Role] — [Specific outcome]`.
- Banner: one visual message; no clutter; text ≤ 8 words.
- Featured section: 2–4 items, each pointing to proof (case study, article, media).
- About section: problem → method → proof → CTA.
- External links in posts go in the **first comment**, never the body.

## Website

- Hero: one offer, one primary CTA, no competing buttons.
- Calendly / booking link must be visible within one scroll on every page.
- About page: 70% client problem and outcome, 30% founder story.
- No blog unless there is a 90-day publishing commitment.

## YouTube channel

- Channel name / handle: consistent with other handles; searchable by target buyer.
- Banner: readable at all sizes; text ≤ 6 words; update quarterly.
- Channel trailer: 30–60 seconds; states the promise and who it is for.
- About section: one-line offer, upload cadence promise, one funnel link.
- Thumbnails: consistent style (fonts, colors, layout); ≤ 5 words; high contrast.
- Playlists: organized by buyer problem, not by content type.

## Cross-channel consistency

- Same name, same photo, same color palette, same offer everywhere.
- Tone: expert but plain-spoken. No jargon for jargon’s sake.
- Profile pictures: face-forward, well-lit, same image across LinkedIn, YouTube, X, and email.

## Anti-patterns

- Different headlines on LinkedIn and the website.
- "We do everything" messaging.
- Banners with stock imagery and no message.
- YouTube thumbnails that look like every other channel in the niche.
- Hiding the booking link behind multiple clicks.
