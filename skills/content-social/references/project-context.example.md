## Operator — who runs this business

**LogicBison** is a solo boutique software studio run by Alejandro Castro (36 years of engineering experience, 3+ years deep in AI-assisted development). It is **not** an agency, not a consultancy, and not a team-for-hire.

Core value proposition: **Strategy + Architecture + Delivery — one person, one result, no handoffs.**

Website: https://logicbison.com

## Target buyer (for social content)

- **Primary:** Non-technical founders and CEOs of seed-stage companies (5–20 people, funded or building).
- **LinkedIn titles:** Founder, CEO, CTO, VP Engineering, Product Lead.
- **Pain vocabulary:** vendor lock-in, scope creep, rebuild, agency handoff, technical co-founder, MVP timeline.
- **Content preference:** Concrete numbers, architecture tradeoffs, real project stories, contrarian-but-true takes on agencies vs. builders.

## Service model

- **Pricing:** Fixed-price, milestone-gated. $20K–$25K MVP · $30K–$80K full platform. 50% upfront.
- **Timeline:** 6–12 weeks to MVP.
- **Differentiator:** One point of accountability. CTO-level judgment + hands-on delivery. No account managers. No handoffs.

## Brand voice

- **Direct, confident, no fluff.** Does not hedge.
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "1.05M rows" not "massive scale."
- **Anti-agency.** Explicitly positions against agencies, consultants, and freelancers.
- **Builder identity.** "I don't just advise. I build."
- **Respect for the reader's intelligence.** No dumbing down; audience is technical-adjacent founders.

## Audience pain points

1. Fear of vendor lock-in / scope creep / runaway costs.
2. Don't know enough to evaluate technical candidates.
3. Have been burned by agencies that handed off the work.
4. Need an MVP fast but can't afford to rebuild it 18 months later.
5. Worried about AI-generated code being maintainable.

## Technology / topic territory

- AI-assisted development (real tradeoffs, not hype)
- Platform architecture decisions for early-stage companies
- How to evaluate a technical partner as a non-technical founder
- FastAPI vs alternatives, Next.js patterns, multi-tenant SaaS design
- RAG, voice AI, LLM integration in production
- Fixed-price vs time-and-material — when each model works

## Content pillars

1. **Architecture lesson** — "Here is a pattern I used on a real project and when it breaks."
2. **AI in practice** — "How I actually use AI in delivery without sacrificing code quality."
3. **Experience insight** — "What 36 years of building taught me about X."
4. **Project BTS** — Behind-the-scenes of a current or recent build.
